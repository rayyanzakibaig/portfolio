American EMR

See Website

Context

  • American EMR is a cloud-based software solution designed to optimize practice management, catering to the diverse needs of medical specialties. The platform’s primary objective is to enhance operational efficiency, organizational structure, and patient care for medical practices.
  • The current EMR (Electronic Medical Record) softwares in the market have these common problems.
  • • Not user friendly, complicated to use, difficult to navigate, and unnecessary noise.
  • • Expensive with limited features; no all in one solution for a healthcare practice.
  • • Outdated

Overview

  • Timeline: 3 months
  • My Role: UX/UI Designer
  • Tools Used: Figma
  • Objective: Design a patient portal, easy to navigate.

Problem Statement

  • Both patients and healthcare professionals have difficulty navigating through electronic medical records and patient portals.
Goals
  • • Create an intuitive, easy-to-use patient portal.
  • • Any user should be able to navigate where they want within 3 clicks.
  • • Streamline navigation for an easy user experience.

Design Process

The Investate Holdings website was crafted using a systematic and user-focused design process to ensure both business goals and user needs were met effectively.

1. Research & Discovery
  • I first gathered info on who Investate is seeking to target with this website, asking what their biggest risks and drawbacks are with their building units. I came to find that a lot of units are vacant, and of the ones that are leased to tenants, there's no telling what the condition of the unit will be once they move out.
    • 2. User Personas
  • I interviewed people in the medical field and asked them about their experience using EPIC, the most popular patient portal software, which has a very outdated user interface.

    • Ideation & Wireframing

      Information Architecture
    • Structured the site with four key sections: Home, About Us, Why Choose Us, Contact.
    • Prioritized ease of navigation with a sticky menu, one page scroll website, and CTAs.
      • Wireframing
    • Created quick low-fidelity wireframes on paper to explore layouts and functionality. Focused on concisely conveying the business model for clients.

    Visual Design

      Branding
    • Incorporated the brand’s black and white color scheme to convey trust and professionalism.
    • Typography: Montserrat, sans-serif fonts for readability and modern aesthetic.
      • Responsive Design
    • Created designs optimized for mobile, tablet, and desktop views.

    Prototyping and Testing

        Usability Testing
      • Version 1
      • I initially wanted to use this design but found that with the depth of information I had, it caused too long of a paragraph under each photo and in order to make it look acceptable, I was having to make too many compromises like making both the subheading and paragraph text much smaller than preferred, which caused readability issues.
      • Version 2
      • After conducting A/B testing, I decided to use a 3-row layout with two columns per row. This design leverages the natural left-to-right reading pattern, ensuring clarity and preventing distractions. By placing the image on the left and text on the right, the reader can easily follow the content without being interrupted, reinforcing the key message before moving to the next point.

    Reflection

    • Challenges I faced:
    • Managing conflicting stakeholder preferences while maintaining user focus.
    • What I learned:
    • Balancing business goals with user needs requires iterative communication and validation.
    • Next Steps:
    • Explore advanced interactivity, such as virtual tours for properties.
    • Incorporate analytics tracking to further optimize the design post-launch.
